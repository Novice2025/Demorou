How a word for "delay" became a slang for agreement.

by Daby

The Secret Meaning of "Demorou"

If you translate it literally, "demorou" means "it took a long time." But in everyday Brazilian Portuguese, the slang has the complete opposite meaning! It's a lively and informal way to agree to something on the spot.

It's the perfect word to show you're on board with an idea without hesitation. Think of it as the English "Sounds good!", "Let's do it!", or "It's on!".

How to Think Like a Brazilian

For an English speaker, the best way to understand "demorou" is to not think about its literal meaning at all. Instead, focus on the feeling and intent behind it.

The expression "demorou" is similar to phrases like:

  • "Say no more!" - The moment you said it, I was already on board.
  • "Done deal!" - The agreement has already been made.
  • "Consider it done!" - The task or plan is already accepted.
It's a verbal shortcut that communicates not just agreement, but enthusiastic and immediate acceptance.
